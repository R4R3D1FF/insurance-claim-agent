Auto Insurance Policy

Policy Number: AUTO-2026-001

Effective Date: January 1, 2026

Section 1 — Definitions
Covered Vehicle means any vehicle specifically listed on the declarations page.
Insured Driver means a driver listed on the policy or a permissive driver authorized by the policyholder.
Collision means impact between the covered vehicle and another vehicle or object.
Comprehensive Loss means damage caused by theft, fire, vandalism, hail, flood, falling objects, or animal collision.
Section 2 — Collision Coverage

The insurer will pay for direct physical damage to a covered vehicle resulting from a collision provided that:

The policy was active on the date of the accident.
The insured driver possessed a valid driver's license.
The accident was reported within 30 days.
The insured cooperates during the investigation.

The maximum payable amount is the actual cash value of the vehicle immediately before the accident, less the applicable deductible.

Section 3 — Collision Exclusions

Collision coverage does not apply if:

the vehicle was used for organized racing,
the driver was operating under the influence of alcohol or illegal drugs,
the vehicle was intentionally damaged,
the vehicle was used to commit a criminal offense,
damage resulted from normal wear and tear or mechanical failure.
Section 4 — Comprehensive Coverage

Comprehensive coverage applies to losses caused by:

theft,
vandalism,
fire,
hail,
flood,
earthquake,
falling trees,
collision with animals.

The policyholder must report theft to the police within 24 hours.

Section 5 — Rental Vehicle Coverage

Rental reimbursement is available only if:

collision or comprehensive coverage applies,
the vehicle is undergoing covered repairs,
receipts are provided.

Maximum reimbursement is $40 per day for 30 days.

Section 6 — Medical Payments

Medical expenses are covered up to $10,000 per injured occupant when:

injuries resulted directly from a covered accident,
treatment begins within 14 days of the accident.

Medical expenses are not covered for injuries sustained while committing a felony.

Section 7 — Deductibles
Coverage	Deductible
Collision	$500
Comprehensive	$250

The deductible is subtracted from each covered loss.

Section 8 — Required Documentation

To process a claim, the insurer may require:

completed claim form,
photographs of vehicle damage,
police report (when applicable),
repair estimate,
proof of ownership.

Failure to provide requested documentation may delay claim processing.

Section 9 — Claim Reporting

The policyholder must notify the insurer:

within 30 days for collision claims,
within 7 days for theft claims,
immediately when fraud is suspected.
Section 10 — Fraud

Coverage may be denied if the claimant knowingly:

submits false documents,
exaggerates damages,
conceals material facts,
submits duplicate claims.
